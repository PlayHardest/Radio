Net Controls

The ARSB sponsors a Sunday morning net on 7.185 Mhz at 12:30z. The Net Control Operator is Ron, 8P6JB. It also hosts a VHF net on one of the two repeaters - on Wednesday evenings at 00:00z or 8:00pm local time on 146.910MHz, -600KHz input. This net is hosted by Rodney, 8P6FH. The ARSB is also responsible for organising net control stations for the Caribbean Emergency and Weather Net when it is Barbados's turn to act as net control. This net meets at 1030Z and 2230Z daily on 3.815MHz. During the morning session however, if conditions are poor on 80m, it convenes on 7.162MHz.

Equipment

The operations centre is equipped with an Icom IC-738 as the main transceiver and an Icom IC-730 as back-up. Antennas include a KLM KT34XA on a 45ft. self-supporting tower. An evolving array of vertical and wire antennas are available for operation on 30, 40 & 80 metres. Two VHF transceivers round out the modest station. One is used for FM voice while the other is dedicated as a 1200baud packet digipeater on 145.010 MHz.

Ham Repeaters

The two ham repeaters on the island are open and enjoy nearly island-wide coverage:

  • 146.910MHz, -600KHz input: The main repeater is located at Sturges a stone's throw from the Harrison's Cave, a major tourist attraction on the island. It is hosted at the transmitter site of the Caribbean Broadcasting Corporation which runs the country's only Television station.
  • 145.310MHz, -600KHz input: The secondary repeater is linked to another repeater in Grenada providing extensive coverage to the islands of St. Vincent & the Grenadines, Trinidad & Tobago and if conditions are good, to Venezeula and as far south as Brazil. This repeater is located at Mount Misery, a microwave site of Cable & Wireless, who are the sole international telecommunications providers on the island.
  • Coming soon!! We are in the process of implementing a major VHF link to the island of Domi which when complete will provide VHF coverage through a series of linked repeaters and hubs from Antigua in the north to Trinidad & Tobago in the south.
